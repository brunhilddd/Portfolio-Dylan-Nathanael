Selected Work

Freelance Project

Multigrosir Inventory Management System & Online Grosir applications

The Problem

User: Medium-scale business owners running wholesale and online sales

Pain Point: Business owners could not monitor stock levels in real time, High risk of overstocking and stockouts, Manual inventory updates consumed time and caused human error, Customers found it difficult to purchase items due to unclear availability

Why It Matters: Inefficient inventory management slows down operations, increases errors, and directly affects revenue. Without a proper system, business owners lose control, while customers lose trust and convenience.

Role & Scope

Role: Lead UI/UX Designer

Team: Solo designer, collaborated with 4 developers and 1 project manager

Type: Team project (12-month timeline)

Research & Analysis

  • Conducted user interviews with 8 business owners to understand inventory workflows and wholesale buying behavior
  • Identified major inefficiencies in manual stock tracking and order handling
  • Analyzed user flows for both admin (store owners) and buyers (customers)
  • Defined data hierarchy focusing on stock availability, product categories, and order volume
  • Mapped system logic to ensure the design aligned with backend inventory processes

Solution

Designed an inventory management system and wholesale ordering experience that prioritizes efficiency, clarity, and scalability. Key design decisions:

  • Centralized inventory dashboard with real-time stock visibility
  • Structured product catalog to simplify bulk purchasing
  • Clear stock indicators to prevent ordering unavailable items
  • Streamlined workflows for managing products, stock updates, and orders
  • Reduced operational friction by minimizing unnecessary steps in daily tasks

Result & Outcome

80% Faster task completion
4.8/5 Client satisfaction
92% User approval rating

Inventory management became faster and more reliable, Business owners gained better control over stock and operations, Customers experienced a smoother and more transparent purchasing process

Team Project

Sokrates School Information Systems & Learning Management Systems

The Problem

User: Students, Teachers, Parents, and Admin

Pain Point: Inefficient user flow across academic services and learning features, overloaded menus with unclear hierarchy, difficulty managing schedules, sessions, and academic data in one place, limited scalability as new features were added, manual verification needed due to unclear system feedback.

Why It Matters: Poor flow and information structure increase cognitive load and reduce system adoption. In an educational environment, this directly affects productivity, learning experience, and data accuracy.

Role & Scope

Role: System Analyst Intern

Team: Worked with 8 developers, 4 system analysts, 1 project manager

Type: Real-world client project (1 year timeline)

Research & Analysis

  • Conducted interviews with school stakeholders to understand daily academic workflows
  • Audited existing user flows to identify friction points and redundant steps
  • Analyzed navigation structure and information architecture
  • Defined role-based needs for admin, teacher, student, and parent
  • Collaborated with developers to align UX improvements with system constraints

Solution

Redesigned the system into a clear, role-based super app that integrates academic services and LMS into a cohesive experience.

  • Simplified academic and learning workflows by reducing unnecessary steps and clarifying system feedback
  • Improved layout and hierarchy for academic modules to make data input, review, and monitoring more intuitive
  • Introduced a structured menu system to support system scalability and feature growth
  • Streamlined access to classes, materials, assignments, and assessments for teachers and students
  • Conducted usability testing and assisted in system testing to ensure flows worked as intended and met user expectations

Result & Outcome

90% Reduction in user friction
95% Reduction in system complexity
5/5 Staff satisfaction

Staff reported the system was "a game-changer" and customers appreciated the ease of booking.

Concept Project

E-Commerce Website & Inventory Management System

The Problem

User: Customer and sellers

Pain Point: Existing finance apps were either too complex or too simplistic, failing to educate sellers while helping them manage money.

Why It Matters: Financial literacy gap leads to poor money management and stress.

Role & Scope

Role: Solo UI/UX Designer

Team: Independent concept project

Type: Portfolio/concept work (self-initiated)

Research & Analysis

  • Surveyed 50+ store owners about inventory management
  • Analyzed competitor apps to identify gaps
  • Created user journey maps for different financial goals
  • Designed information architecture balancing simplicity and depth

Solution

Created a progressive disclosure approach that grows with user knowledge:

  • Onboarding that assesses inventory management level
  • Contextual education embedded in the interface
  • Visual budget tracking with actionable insights
  • Goal-based inventory management

Result & Outcome

15+ Positive peer reviews
2 Client inquiries generated
Featured In design community

The concept demonstrated my ability to balance educational content with usability, leading to new client opportunities.
